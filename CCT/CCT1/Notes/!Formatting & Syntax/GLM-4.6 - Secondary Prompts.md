**Note generation**
Answer the following questions:
- Does the generated note conform to the given instruction set?
	- Was all pictures preserved?
	- Have any explicitly stated instructions been forgotten?
	- Was the Figure text generated, and formatted in the correct place?
	- Were appropriate diagrams generated to explain and deepen understanding of the topic(s)?

### Evaluation Checklist

- **Goal 1: Deep Understanding**
    - [ ]  Are complex concepts rephrased in clear language?
    - [ ]  Is context provided ("why is this important?")?
    - [ ]  Are concepts linked to each other?
    - [ ]  **Is an appropriate example provided for every new or complex concept?**
    - [ ]  **Are mini-examples (`>[!example]`) used for simple concepts and for complex procedures?**
    - [ ]  **Are callouts used strategically to structure the information and guide the reader's focus?**
- **Goal 2: Self-Sufficiency**
    - [ ]  Is all essential information from the source included?
    - [ ]  Are all key terms and acronyms defined?
    - [ ]  Is the note structured for easy review?
- **Goal 3: Readability**
    - [ ]  Is the tone clear and appropriate for a classmate?
    - [ ]  Is formatting consistent throughout?
    - [ ]  Is the information factually accurate?
- **Document Structure**
    - [ ]  Is the quick reference table present (if appropriate)?
- **Formatting and Visuals**
    - [ ]  Are callouts, bold, italics, and code used correctly?
    - [ ]  **Is there a variety of callout types used appropriately (e.g., `info`, `example`, `question`, `warning`)?**
    - [ ]  Are figures numbered and captioned correctly?
    - [ ]  Do tables have figure text with numbering and captions?
    - [ ]  Are pasted images from the source text preserved?
    - [ ]  If diagrams are used, are they simple, clear, and syntactically correct?
- **Subject-Specific Guidelines**
    - [ ]  Does the content align with the guidelines for the specific subject (e.g., technical, theoretical)?
